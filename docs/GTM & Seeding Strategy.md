# GTM & Seeding Strategy — The Tribunal

## Goal

Create repeatable early acquisition without depending on a single domain.

## Dual-lane launch plan

### Lane A: Politics/news (virality)

- Source: trending claims, breaking news, OSINT threads
- Content format: fast, punchy, screenshot-friendly
- Success metric: shares and new judges per claim

### Lane B: Religion/theology (meaning)

- Source: communities that already debate proofs
- Content format: longer evidence chains, “unresolved” as honest outcome
- Success metric: repeat sessions, judge streaks, evidence depth

## Seeding playbook

- Start with 10–30 claims total.
- For each claim, seed 6–12 evidence items:
    - 2–3 high-credibility sources
    - 2–3 low-credibility but popular sources (so the split is visible)
    - 2–3 “strong logic / weak source” items
    - 2–3 “strong source / weak inference” items

## Event format: Tribunal Nights

- 2–3 sessions per week
- 1 claim per lane + 1 wildcard
- Each session produces a recap artifact:
    - Verdict card
    - Biggest mover
    - Wildest split
    - MVP judge

## Community onboarding

- Bring in 20–50 seed judges.
- Give a simple onboarding path:
    - Watch 1 example split
    - Judge 3 items
    - Share 1 card

## Channel experiments

- X (primary): screenshot culture
- Discord: live session coordination
- Substack embeds: persistent distribution

## Distribution detail: make [tribunal.so](http://tribunal.so) self-teaching

### The .so framing

Use the rhetorical device in copy where it fits:

- **“[tribunal.so](http://tribunal.so) — so, what’s your verdict?”**
- **“Tribunal. So… judge it.”**
- Every share artifact should include [**tribunal.so**](http://tribunal.so) so the URL is learned by exposure.
- During Tribunal Nights, display `tribunal.so/<short-path>` prominently on screen and in recaps.
- Prefer copy that doesn’t require spelling the TLD:
    - “Judge it on [**tribunal.so**](http://tribunal.so).”
    - “Verdict: [**tribunal.so/c/<claim>**](http://tribunal.so/c/<claim>).”

## What we will not do early

- Broad paid marketing
- SEO-first strategy (until we have enough claim pages)

## Stack implication (distribution-driven)

The product’s growth model is URL-based virality: someone sees a score card on X and lands on `tribunal.so/c/<claim>` and can judge immediately. Avoid any early choice (especially native-first) that adds install friction or breaks link sharing.
